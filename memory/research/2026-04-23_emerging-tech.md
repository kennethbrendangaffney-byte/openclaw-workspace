## Quantum Computing

### Major Milestones
- **ParityQC 52-Qubit Quantum Fourier Transform (April 16, 2026):** European quantum architecture company ParityQC demonstrated the largest Quantum Fourier Transform ever reported, using 52 superconducting qubits on IBM hardware. This is a significant benchmark for quantum algorithms.
- **Fermionic Atom Gates Exceed 99% Accuracy (April 20, 2026):** A long-sought milestone achieved — fermionic atom quantum gates now top 99% accuracy, bringing neutral atom quantum computing closer to fault-tolerant operation.
- **Rigetti 108-Qubit System (April 7, 2026):** Rigetti announced general availability of Cepheus-1-108Q, validating their chiplet-based scaling architecture for modular quantum processors.
- **Protected Quantum Gates in Optical Lattices:** New research in *Nature* demonstrates protected quantum gates using qubit doublons in dynamical optical lattices, advancing neutral atom platforms.

### Key Trend
Quantum computing is transitioning from pure research to engineering challenges — scaling, error correction, and commercial availability are the new frontiers.

## Biotechnology & Gene Editing

### CRISPR Advances
- **In-Body Gene Editing at 90% Efficiency (April 13, 2026):** A compact CRISPR system (Cas12f) enables targeted in-body gene editing with up to 90% efficiency, opening the door to AAV-delivered therapies without extracting cells.
- **Cut-Free Gene Editing:** New gene editors skip DNA breaks entirely, reducing off-target risks and unintended mutations — a major safety improvement.
- **Photoactivatable CRISPR/Cas13d:** Using upconversion nanoparticles for deep tissue RNA engineering and orthopedic therapy, enabling light-controlled gene editing inside the body.
- **CRISPR-Enhanced Cancer Immune Cells:** CRISPR-Cas9 used to engineer safer, more effective cancer-fighting immune cells directly inside mice — a step toward in-vivo CAR-T therapy.
- **Multiplex Genome Editing:** Engineered Un1Cas12f1 enables multiplex editing with enhanced activity and broader targeting scope.

### Key Trend
Gene editing is moving from ex-vivo (cells edited outside body) to in-vivo (edited inside the body), with improved precision and safety. The era of "gene editing as medicine" is accelerating.

## Novel Materials

### Graphene & Energy Storage
- **Graphene Metamaterials for Terahertz Modulation:** UK/German researchers achieved full terahertz amplitude modulation using graphene metamaterials — enabling advanced communications and sensing.
- **Laser-Engraved Graphene Batteries:** Ultra-light freestanding lithium-free anodes for lithium batteries using laser-engraved graphene architecture.
- **CO₂-Etched Porous Graphene:** Novel conductive additive for high-performance LiNi₀.₈Co₀.₁Mn₀.₁O₂ batteries, improving energy density.
- **Multiscale Curved Graphene Supercapacitors:** Operando interlayer expansion enables volumetrically-efficient supercapacitors — higher energy density in compact form factors.
- **Graphene Nanocomposites Review:** Comprehensive advances in design and electrochemical mechanisms for high-performance energy storage.

### Key Trend
Graphene is finally finding its commercial niche in energy storage — batteries and supercapacitors are the first major applications where graphene's properties translate to real performance gains.
