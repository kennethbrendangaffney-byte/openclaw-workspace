## 🔬 Quantum Computing

**Limited direct quantum news this cycle**, but related developments in AI-chip design show the broader compute landscape:

- **Agentic AI Chip Design:** Verkor.io's "Design Conductor" system designed a complete RISC-V CPU core (VerCore, 1.48 GHz) in just **12 hours** using only a 219-word prompt. This represents a major milestone in AI-automated hardware design — the first fully AI-designed RISC-V core. Performance comparable to a 2011 Intel Celeron, but the trajectory is what's significant: "If it can't do it today, it'll do it in six months."
- **RAM Crisis:** A global memory shortage is affecting GPUs and AI hardware. Nvidia launched a 12GB RTX 5070 laptop GPU variant using 24Gb G7 memory to maximise availability during the shortage.

## 🧬 Biotechnology & CRISPR

**Major regulatory breakthrough for personalised medicine:**

- **FDA "Plausible Mechanism Pathway" (Feb 2026):** The US FDA proposed a radical streamlining of personalised CRISPR therapy approvals. Under the new framework, drug companies can treat many people with different mutations in a **single clinical trial** — as long as participants share the same clinical symptoms.
- **Timeline collapse:** Approval time could drop from ~4 years to **as little as 3 months**, with costs falling from $25M+ to **under $250,000 per patient**.
- **First success story:** Baby KJ Muldoon (Feb 2025) was the first person to receive a personalised CRISPR therapy for a life-threatening metabolic disorder. The FDA customised its approach to fast-track approval.
- **On-demand CRISPR:** If early trials succeed, the FDA could approve "CRISPR-on-demand" — physicians could prescribe personalised gene editors directly to patients without individual trials for each mutation.
- **350 million people** worldwide live with one of 5,000+ genetic diseases that could theoretically be treated this way.
- **GLP-1 drug expansion:** Research suggests Ozempic/Zepbound's greatest benefit may be their **anti-inflammatory power**, beyond just appetite and blood sugar control.

## 🚀 Space Technology

**Moon race heating up — US vs China:**

- **NASA Artemis II:** Targeting launch in **March 2026** (delayed from 2024/2025). Four astronauts (including first Canadian, Jeremy Hansen) will orbit the Moon for the first time in 50+ years. Uses proven shuttle-era technology — four RS-25 engines with histories dating back to 1981.
- **Artemis III restructuring:** NASA added an extra test flight in 2027 — Orion will rendezvous with lunar landing craft in low Earth orbit before any actual Moon landing. First lunar landing now pushed to **Artemis IV or V in 2028**.
- **China competition:** Multiple voices warning that China's space agency may beat Artemis to the Moon. NASA faces staff/budget cuts and leadership changes under the Trump administration.
- **Mars organic molecules:** NASA's Curiosity rover detected **21 organic compounds** in Gale Crater rocks (7 never seen before on Mars), including nitrogen heterocycles — precursors to DNA/RNA. The discovery confirms Mars can preserve ancient biosignatures, though origin (biological vs geological) remains unknown.
- **Meta's space-based solar:** Energy deal for 24/7 orbit-to-grid solar power using satellites in geosynchronous orbit (~22,000 miles up) beaming energy to ground solar farms as near-infrared light.
- **Data centres in space:** Growing discussion about launching data centres into orbit to bypass terrestrial controversies and cooling constraints.

## 🤖 Robotics & AI

**Humanoid robots hitting real-world milestones:**

- **Beijing Half-Marathon (April 2026):** Humanoid robots **beat human runners** in a 21km race. 100+ robots competed alongside 12,000 people — three robots crossed the finish line ahead of any human. Marks a breakthrough in China's rapidly advancing robotics industry.
- **Table tennis robot:** Sony AI's "Ace" robot (featured on Nature cover) uses high-speed perception to predict complex ball movements and compete against elite players.
- **Juggling robot:** AthenaZero learns to juggle barehanded using only on-board sensory feedback — no motion capture, no funnels, no human assistance adding the third ball. Adapts to contact uncertainties through hand-eye coordination learning.
- **Unitree robots at Haneda Airport:** Japan Airlines trialling Unitree robots for luggage/cargo movement (May 2026), with plans for cabin cleaning and permanent deployment to address Japan's labour shortage.
- **Quadruped patrol robots:** DEEP Robotics deploying quadruped robots for 24/7 residential community patrol in North America.
- **World Models:** A major AI trend — systems trained on real-world physics data (not just text/images) that generate interactive 3D environments. Nvidia's Cosmos, Google's Genie 3, Runway's GWM-1. AMI Labs raised **>$1 billion** for world model development. Key for robotics and self-driving training.
- **Ringbot Quad:** Novel monocycle robot with four legs combining wheeled and legged locomotion — switches between driving and walking modes for versatile terrain navigation.

## 🧪 Novel Materials & Energy

**Fusion energy takes a step toward the grid:**

- **Commonwealth Fusion Systems** applied to join PJM Interconnection (largest US wholesale electricity market) — **first fusion company to enter a grid operator's queue**. Aiming for first power plant (ARC) in Virginia by early 2030s. SPARC demonstration reactor planned for 2027.
- **Germany's Wendelstein 7-X:** Demonstrated containing superheated plasma for **43 seconds**. Joint European Torus achieved ~1 minute before retirement in 2023.
- **Rotating detonation rocket engines:** Astrobotic testing "ring of fire" engines that maximise thrust while using less fuel — could help spacecraft explore farther in the solar system.
- **Soft electrostatic actuators:** Max Planck Institute demonstrated efficiencies up to **63.6%** (3x higher than previously reported) using Peano-HASEL actuators — paving way for more energy-efficient soft robotic systems.
- **Microplastics in brain tissue:** Research found relatively high levels of micro- and nanoplastics around brain tumours, suggesting blood-brain barrier breakdown — raises concerns about plastic exposure and neurological health.
